<p><p>The non-invasive sphygmomanometer market consists of three primary types: automatic, mercury, and aneroid sphygmomanometers. Automatic sphygmomanometers use digital technology for quick, accurate readings, making them user-friendly and suitable for home use. Mercury sphygmomanometers are traditional devices known for their precision, though they are declining in popularity due to health concerns associated with mercury. Aneroid sphygmomanometers rely on a dial and manual inflation, offering accuracy but requiring training for proper use. Each type serves distinct healthcare needs and settings.</p></p>
