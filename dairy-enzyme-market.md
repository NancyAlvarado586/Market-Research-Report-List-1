<p><p>The dairy enzyme market comprises various types essential for cheese and dairy production. Lactase is used to break down lactose, making dairy products digestible for lactose-intolerant consumers. Chymosin, derived from calves or produced microbially, is crucial for coagulating milk in cheese-making. Microbial rennet, a vegetarian alternative, is gaining popularity as it caters to diverse dietary restrictions. Lipase enhances flavor profiles in cheeses through fat breakdown. The "Others" category includes additional enzymes like proteases and amylases, contributing to various dairy applications.</p></p>
