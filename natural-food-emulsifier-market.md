<p><p>The natural food emulsifier market includes several key types. Lecithin, derived from soy or eggs, acts as a lubricant and stabilizer in foods. Lanolin, obtained from sheep wool, offers moisture retention properties, enhancing texture. Gum Arabic, a plant-derived hydrocolloid, serves as a thickener and stabilizer, widely used in beverages and confections. Other emulsifiers encompass various plant and animal sources that help blend ingredients, improve shelf life, and enhance the overall quality of food products.</p></p>
