<p><p>The craft beer market is primarily divided into two main categories: ales and lagers. Ales, fermented at warmer temperatures, tend to have a more complex flavor profile, often featuring fruity and spicy notes. They include styles like pale ales and IPAs. Lagers, fermented at cooler temperatures, are typically crisper and cleaner, showcasing a smooth texture with subtle flavors; popular styles include pilsners and amber lagers. Both categories cater to diverse consumer preferences, driving innovation and growth in the craft beer industry.</p></p>
