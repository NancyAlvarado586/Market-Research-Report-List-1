<p><p>The edible thickener market consists of various types, including animal glue, vegetable gum, seaweed gum, microbial glue, and starch. Animal glue, derived from collagen, is often used in desserts and sauces. Vegetable gums, such as guar and xanthan, provide texture in baked goods. Seaweed gums, like carrageenan and alginate, are essential for dairy and meat products. Microbial glue, produced through fermentation, offers innovative thickening solutions. Starch, derived from plants, is widely utilized for its binding properties and versatility in food applications.</p></p>
