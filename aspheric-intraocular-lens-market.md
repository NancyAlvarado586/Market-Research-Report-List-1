<p><p>The aspheric intraocular lens market is primarily divided into two types: rigid and foldable lenses. Rigid aspheric lenses are made from hard materials and maintain their shape, offering stability and precise optical performance, commonly used in cataract surgeries. In contrast, foldable aspheric lenses are designed for minimally invasive procedures, allowing them to be folded and injected through smaller incisions. This flexibility enhances patient recovery and reduces surgical complications, making foldable lenses increasingly popular among surgeons and patients alike.</p></p>
