<p><p>The high-duty refractory market is primarily divided into clay-based and non-clay-based types. Clay-based refractories, derived from natural clays, offer excellent thermal stability and are widely used in steel, aluminum, and glass industries. Non-clay-based refractories, including those made from materials like alumina, silica, and magnesia, provide superior resistance to high temperatures and chemical erosion, making them suitable for industrial applications such as cement and petrochemical processing. Both types cater to specific thermal management and durability needs across various sectors.</p></p>
